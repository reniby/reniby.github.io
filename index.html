About Me

Emma Waddell is a computer scientist and musician studying at NYU Gallatin. Their concentration is focused on how natural and biological processes influence algorithmic and computer music. This includes simulations and the use of neural networks, as well as the study of communication from a musical/visual and linguistic standpoint.
They have received multiple research grants to study these ideas, and create new music using their computer and their saxophone. Additionally they have studied extensive music theory through jazz saxophone and classical piano. They are also interested in video game development and woodworking!

Look through this portfolio to read about their research and listen to some notable live performances.

Computer Music Research 2018-2022

Bird Ring

Composed an album of algorithmic music based on ornithological data, using Java, Max/MSP, and p5.js. Created an interactive website and art piece using HTML/CSS and Javascript to host live simulations, music videos, and educational readings based on this album. Presented this project as a featured artist in the PHREATIC! exhibition on Governor's Island. This project was initially funded by the NYU Gallatin Undergraduate Research Fund.

Website » PHREATIC! »

Creative Neural Networks

Created a procedurally generated platformer game in Unity. User inputs and choices are fed into a creative neural network in Pure Data which generates a live soundtrack for the game that evolves as more user data is sent in. This project was funded by the NYU Gallatin Dean's Award for Summer Research, and Emma was selected as a keynote speaker in the Gallatin Student Resarch Conference to present and speak about this project.

Conference Video » Research Paper » Game Download » Blog Posts »

Essay on Experimental Composition · Coming Soon!

An essay comparing the compositional styles and choices in Igor Stravinsky’s The Rite of Spring and Gertrude Stein’s Tender Buttons. To be published in Spring 2022. Check back for more information!

Computer Science Research 2018-2022

Extractive Summarization

Built an extratctive summarizer using Python, evaluated on ROGUE-1 and ROUGE-L scores. Used the Critical Role Dungeons and Dragons Dataset (CRD3), colleced from 398,682 turns, to generate consice summaries of episodes or shorter scenes. In addition, we utilized an Ad-Hoc Information Retrieval system using TF-IDF weights and cosine similarity score that generated sentences that assist Dungeon and Dragons 5th Edition world-building and narrative creation, based on a defined series of user queries.

Paper » Github » Dataset »

Comparing the Hodgkin Huxley and Traub Neuron Model in MATLAB

Tested the firing rate of action potentials using two mathematical models to reveal information about stimulus strength. Used MATLAB to apply a sustained stimulus at various currents to compare the frequencies of the action potentals between the Hodgkin-Huxley model of a squid giant axon, and the slightly altered Traub neuron.
